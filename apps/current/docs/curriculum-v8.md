# V8 Curriculum Contract

## North-star outcome

The learner can hear an idea, predict and sing its relationships, locate it on
guitar, play it in time, explain what remains constant, transform it, transfer
it to a new context, and preserve it inside an original piece.

## Eight strands

1. Sound production and physical control
2. Pulse, rhythm, metre and groove
3. Fretboard geography and interval structures
4. Ear, audiation and transcription
5. Melody, phrasing and improvisation
6. Chords, harmony and voice leading
7. Composition, arrangement and form
8. Reflection, transfer and independent practice

## Forty-eight units

The path contains six units in each of eight stages: Sound and time; Movable
fretboard map; Tonal hearing and melody; Triads and harmonic movement; Groove
and musical language; Functional and modal harmony; Composition and form; and
Personal voice. It is nominally 240 sessions, but is competency-paced rather
than calendar-gated.

## Required learning loop

Every unit must contain the following typed activities:

```text
Hear → predict → imitate → locate → name → compare → vary → create → transfer → reflect
```

Completion requires an observable musical action or a specific reflection.
There are no generic lesson-completion checkboxes.

## Evidence rules

- Assisted work can complete exposure but does not count as independent mastery.
- `secure` requires successful unassisted work on two separate days.
- `transfer-ready` additionally requires unassisted transfer into a changed key,
  neck region, tempo, instrument or creative context.
- Creation and reflection inform recommendations without receiving a numerical
  creativity score.
- Outside-the-scale material is described contextually rather than marked wrong.
- The starting baseline sets the initial recommended unit only; it grants no mastery records.

## Session balance

The default daily plan is 25 minutes: 3 minutes of sound/reference, 5 of
technique and time, 6 of relationship or ear work, 6 of guitar application,
and 5 of creation or reflection.

## Content review gate

All content must pass automated graph and contract validation, theory review,
guitar-playability review, accessibility review and a real learner trial. A
unit is not publishable when its prose claims an outcome its interaction cannot
produce or observe.
