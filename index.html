Alternative & Non-Western Systems

The matrix you have been studying maps the 12-tone equal temperament (12-TET) system configured specifically for functional Western harmony. However, treating this as the universal truth is a category error.

Atonal / 12-Tone Serialism

In the early 20th century, composers like Arnold Schoenberg dismantled the concept of a "Tonic" or home key. In 12-tone serialism, all 12 notes of the chromatic scale are treated with absolute equality. You cannot use the fretboard to map "tension" and "resolution" here, because tension requires a sovereign centre to pull against. In serialism, there is no gravity; you are floating in deep space.

Microtonal & Non-Western (Ragas & Maqams)

Indian Classical Ragas and Arabic Maqams do not merely use different notes; they divide the octave into smaller intervals (microtones or shrutis) that do not exist on the standard guitar fretboard.

More importantly, these systems are not just scales—they dictate specific melodic movements, ornamental rules, and emotional or temporal contexts (e.g., a Raga meant only to be played at dawn). Forcing these systems onto a Western grid fundamentally strips them of their architectural integrity.
